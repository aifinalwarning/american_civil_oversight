How a Bill Becomes Law

The U.S. legislative process is designed to be deliberate and thorough. Understanding how a bill becomes law helps you identify the best opportunities to make your voice heard.

The Legislative Journey

Every law starts as an idea. A member of Congress drafts the idea into a bill, which then goes through a multi-step process before potentially becoming law.

Pro Tip: Best Time to Act

The committee stage is often the most influential time for citizen input. Most bills die in committee, so contacting your representatives before committee votes can have significant impact.
